Access to information and personal information request system

The Access to Information Act and the Privacy Act give you the right to access information and records held by government institutions.

Canadian citizens, permanent residents and any person or corporation present in Canada can request two types of information from the government:

  1. Records related to the daily operations of government institutions, such as policies, reports, plans, communications, etc. (Available under the Access to Information Act)
  2. Personal information about individuals, held by government institutions for the purposes of administering programs and services. (Available under the Privacy Act)

You can make both government and personal information requests using this request system. Learn more about making a request.
